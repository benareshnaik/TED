India Marks Major Space Milestone with PSLV-C62 Launch

India achieved a significant milestone in its space journey as Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) successfully launched PSLV-C62 carrying the advanced EOS-N1 along with 18 secondary payloads. The launch took place from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre in Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh.

The EOS-N1 satellite is designed to strengthen India’s earth-observation capabilities by providing high-resolution imagery for agriculture monitoring, disaster management, urban planning, border surveillance, and environmental studies. The 18 co-passenger satellites include domestic and international payloads, highlighting India’s growing role in the global commercial launch market.

The PSLV rocket, known for its reliability, once again demonstrated precision deployment of multiple satellites into designated orbits. This mission reinforces India’s reputation as a cost-effective and dependable space partner while enhancing national security and scientific research capabilities.

IN BRIEF IV

Upcoming Solar Eclipse: India prepares for the first solar eclipse of 2026 on Feb 17, featuring a ‘ring of fire’ appearance, sparking interest among skywatchers and astrologers nationwide

India is gearing up for the first solar eclipse of 2026, set to occur on February 17, an event that is already generating widespread excitement among astronomers, skywatchers, and astrologers. The eclipse is expected to display a spectacular “ring of fire” effect—an annular solar eclipse—where the Moon passes between the Earth and the Sun but does not completely cover it. As a result, a bright ring of sunlight remains visible around the dark silhouette of the Moon.

Scientific institutions, including the Indian Space Research Organisation, have encouraged citizens to observe the phenomenon safely using certified solar viewing glasses or indirect viewing methods. Planetariums and science centers across major cities are preparing special observation sessions and awareness programs to educate the public about the science behind eclipses.

Beyond its scientific significance, the eclipse has also sparked discussions in cultural and astrological circles, with many people following traditional practices associated with such celestial events. Experts, however, stress the importance of relying on scientific guidance for safe viewing. The rare celestial display is expected to draw large crowds, making it one of the most anticipated astronomical events of the year in India.

Bernoulli’s Principle

Bernoulli’s Principle states that as the speed of a moving fluid increases, the pressure within that fluid decreases.

In simpler terms: Fast-moving air creates low pressure.

Difficult Words from Today's News

  1. Access-controlled
    Context: Used to describe the ₹3,175 crore highway upgrade in Telangana.
    Meaning: A type of highway design where entry and exit are strictly regulated via specific ramps or interchanges, rather than allowing any road or driveway to connect to it. This is done to maintain high speeds and safety.

  2. Deliberate
    Context: Used regarding the student finalists at the National Environment Youth Parliament.
    Meaning: To engage in long and careful consideration or discussion before making a decision. In this context, it refers to the formal, structured debate students used to tackle environmental issues.

  3. Afforestation
    Context: Mentioned as a strategy for forest conservation by the students in Nagpur.
    Meaning: The process of establishing a forest on land that has not recently been covered with trees. It is the opposite of deforestation and differs from reforestation (which is replanting trees where a forest used to be).

  4. Annular
    Context: Used to describe the "ring of fire" solar eclipse occurring on February 17.
    Meaning: Derived from the Latin word annulus (ring). In astronomy, it refers to an eclipse where the Moon is too far from Earth to completely cover the Sun, leaving a thin ring of the Sun’s disc visible.

  5. Trilateral
    Context: Used to describe the naval drill between India, Japan, and Indonesia.
    Meaning: Involving or shared by three parties—in this case, three distinct nations or their military forces.
